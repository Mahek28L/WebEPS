Targeting Options

PPC platforms, such as Google Ads and Bing Ads, offer various targeting options to reach specific audiences based on factors like demographics, location, interests, and behavior.

Keyword Targeting

Advertisers bid on keywords relevant to their products or services. When users search for those keywords, the ads may appear at the top or bottom of the search engine results page (SERP).

AD Rank

Ad position on search engine results pages is determined by a combination of bid amount, ad quality, and relevance. Advertisers with higher ad rank are more likely to have their ads shown in prominent positions.

Remarketing

PPC platforms offer remarketing capabilities, allowing advertisers to target users who have previously visited their website or interacted with their ads, increasing the chances of conversion.

HOW IT WORKS

Designed to be straightforward and collaborative

AD Creation

Advertisers create ads that are relevant to the selected keywords. These ads typically include a headline, a description, and a link to a landing page where users can take further action, such as making a purchase or signing up for a newsletter.

Bidding

Advertisers bid on the selected keywords in a PPC auction system. They specify the maximum amount they are willing to pay for each click on their ad. The bid amount, along with other factors such as ad quality and relevance, determines the ad's position and visibility.

AD Placement

Based on the auction results, the PPC platform selects and displays ads on the search engine results page (SERP) or other relevant websites within its network. Ads are typically displayed above or below the organic search results or alongside content on partner websites.

Tracking & Optimization

Advertisers track the performance of their PPC campaigns using metrics such as clicks,impressions,click-through rates(CTR) and return on investment (ROI).They use this data to optimize their campaigns by adjusting bids, refining targeting, and improving landing pages to maximize results.
